Another useful item here would be the water skin, which can hold much more water than the small cup that was issued in the newbie kit.

Now that we've found some items that we would like to buy we should exit the page break by using q for quit. Items can be bought be either their names or by their number. In this case, Fuor wants to purchase a dark coloured flask full of oil and a water skin by using the buy command.

Another useful item that Fuor should probably buy is a pan, which can be used to cure meat from animals by using the command cook <food> over a fire and thus prolonging it's life compared to that of raw meat which decays rather quickly.

Once again we invoke the list command, but this time we'll use it in a more direct fashion by telling it within what parameters search for within by using the command list pan.


*>list pan
You can buy:

397. nine pans up to 3 silver 12 copper.

*>buy pan
A grocer tells you 'Ah! Good choice! That will be 3 silver and 12 copper.'
You now have a pan.

Fuor spent 4 silver and 23 copper, leaving him with only 1 silver and 23 copper left. The amount left should be adequate for any other uses such as paying the rent.
